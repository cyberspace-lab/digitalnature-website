Rachel and Stephen Kaplan's Attention Restoration Theory describes how prolonged use of **directed attention** in modern life leads to fatigue, irritability, and cognitive errors.

Forest environments engage **involuntary attention**, or *soft fascination* — the mind is captured effortlessly by interesting but non-taxing stimuli: shadows on the forest floor, the movement of leaves, distant birdsong.

For an environment to be fully restorative, ART requires four attributes: *being away* (psychological distance), *extent* (a coherent immersive world), *fascination* (engaging stimuli), and *compatibility* (fit between environment and user needs).

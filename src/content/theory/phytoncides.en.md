Phytoncides are volatile organic compounds (VOCs) produced by trees as secondary metabolites to defend against pathogens and insects. They enter the human bloodstream through the nasal mucous membranes and lungs.

The principal components are monoterpenes: **α-pinene, β-pinene, limonene, and sabinene**. Within one hour of forest exposure, blood levels of pinene can increase sixfold.

Coniferous forests (pine, spruce, fir) emit higher quantities of monoterpenes, while broadleaf species release more isoprene. Concentration depends on temperature, light intensity, and stand density.
